About Me

JA

Building Dreams, Shaping Futures

Born and raised in Kisii, my journey into architecture began with a simple fascination: how spaces shape human experiences. From the rolling hills of my hometown to the classrooms of Kapsabet High School, I observed how environment influences behavior, culture, and opportunity.

This curiosity led me to the Technical University of Kenya (TUK), where I pursued a degree in Architecture. There, I didn't just learn to design buildings—I learned to solve problems, to listen to communities, and to envision futures where structures serve as catalysts for transformation.

Upon graduation, I founded Archabitive, driven by a singular mission: to prove that African architects can compete and excel on the global stage while remaining deeply rooted in local context and community needs.

My Journey

The Beginning - Kisii

Early Years

Growing up in Kisii, I was surrounded by organic architecture—homes built from local materials, structures that responded to climate and terrain. This early exposure taught me that great design doesn't require expensive materials; it requires understanding and respect for context.

Education - Kapsabet to TUK

2012 - 2019

At Kapsabet High School, I excelled in technical drawing and mathematics, finding joy in translating ideas into precise visual representations. This passion propelled me to TUK, where I immersed myself in architectural theory, sustainable design, and urban planning. My final year thesis explored affordable housing solutions for rapidly urbanizing communities—a theme that continues to drive my work today.

Founding Archabitive

2020

Shortly after graduating, I took a leap of faith and founded Archabitive. The name combines "architecture" with "initiative"—reflecting my belief that architects must be proactive problem-solvers, not passive service providers. With a small but talented team, we began taking on projects that others overlooked: small residential renovations, community centers, and micro-commercial spaces. Each project taught us invaluable lessons about client needs, construction realities, and business management.

Growth & Vision

2020 - Present

Today, Archabitive has grown to a team of six specialists, working across residential, commercial, and institutional sectors. But our ambition extends far beyond Kenya's borders. We're actively pursuing international collaborations, showcasing that African architectural firms can deliver world-class design while maintaining competitive pricing and deep cultural insight. Every project we complete is a statement: Africa belongs at the table of global architecture.

Design Philosophy

Context-Driven Design

Every project begins with listening—to the site, the client, and the community. I believe great architecture emerges from deep understanding of local climate, culture, materials, and aspirations. Generic solutions have no place in my practice.

Sustainable Innovation

Sustainability isn't a trend; it's a responsibility. I integrate passive design strategies, local materials, and energy-efficient systems into every project. Modern doesn't mean wasteful—it means intelligent.

Human-Centered Spaces

Buildings are for people. Whether designing a family home or a commercial complex, I prioritize how spaces will be lived in, worked in, and experienced. Aesthetics matter, but functionality and emotional resonance matter more.

Featured Projects

01

The Riverside Residence

Residential | Nairobi, Kenya | 2023

A contemporary family home that seamlessly blends indoor and outdoor living spaces. Designed with large openings to capture natural light and ventilation, the residence features locally sourced stone and timber, creating a warm, sustainable environment. The project demonstrates how modern design can honor traditional Kenyan craftsmanship.

02

Greenview Commercial Plaza

Commercial | Thika, Kenya | 2022

A mixed-use development featuring retail spaces on the ground floor and offices above. The design prioritizes pedestrian experience with covered walkways and landscaped courtyards. Solar panels and rainwater harvesting systems reduce operational costs while supporting environmental sustainability.

03

Hope Primary School Extension

Institutional | Kisii, Kenya | 2021

An expansion project for my hometown's primary school, adding four classrooms and a library. Working within a tight budget, we used local materials and community labor to create bright, well-ventilated learning spaces. The project proves that quality educational infrastructure doesn't require exorbitant budgets—just thoughtful design and community collaboration.

04

Urban Loft Renovation

Residential | Nairobi, Kenya | 2023

Transformation of a 1970s apartment into a modern urban loft. By removing non-structural walls and introducing mezzanine levels, we maximized vertical space in the compact unit. Industrial finishes pay homage to the building's original character while contemporary furnishings create a fresh, livable atmosphere.
